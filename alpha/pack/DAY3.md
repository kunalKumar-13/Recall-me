# Day 3 — does it stick?

If you got here, you've installed, opened the
launcher at least twice, and Recall has been
running for three days.

**The Day 3 question:** are you still opening
the launcher when you sit down? Or did you
forget it existed?

That's the only question we care about right
now. Honest answer wins.

---

## The honest answer matters

We are five people in. If three of you forgot
Recall existed by Day 3, the product is wrong
and we need to know before we ship to 50.

**There is no penalty for "I forgot."** That's
useful data. Write it down:

> "I forgot about Recall on Day 2. Re-opened
> today because the email reminded me. Wouldn't
> have otherwise."

That sentence — verbatim — is the most useful
feedback we can get.

## What we're hoping to see

The two-line success criterion:

1. **You opened the launcher unprompted on at
   least 2 of 3 days.** (Returning behaviour.)
2. **At least one Continue card was right.**
   (Trust earned at least once.)

If both → you stay in the cohort and we
escalate to a Week 1 check-in.

If only #1 → the surface is sticky but recovery
quality is wrong. We dig into the engine.

If only #2 → the wow lands but the daily ritual
doesn't form. We dig into the launcher copy and
hotkey discoverability.

If neither → the experiment is honest evidence
that RC1 isn't ready for a wider release.
That's also a result.

---

## Wow checklist

The directive's bar is **1 wow moment from the
cohort.** A "wow" qualifies if it's a moment
where Recall did something you didn't *quite*
expect — and it was useful.

Examples of what counts:

> "I closed the laptop Friday. Opened Monday.
> Hit Alt+Space. The exact three repos and one
> StackOverflow tab I needed for the bug were
> in one card."

> "Recall surfaced a thread I'd forgotten I was
> in — the proposal draft from Tuesday I'd
> been avoiding. Single click, all tabs open,
> didn't have to think."

If you have one of those, **save it verbatim**
to [`../wow/`](../wow/README.md) — your words,
not ours.

---

## Failure checklist

Examples of what counts as a useful failure:

> "Continue card said *Continue: Hotstar*. I
> wasn't continuing anything. Just watching TV
> last night. Felt like the engine confused
> passive browsing for work."

> "Launcher took 4 seconds to open the first
> time today. After that fast. Don't know why."

> "Extension said *disconnected* after the
> daemon ran for 12 hours. Restarting fixed
> it. Didn't notice for an hour."

Drop them into
[`../failures/`](../failures/README.md). One file
per failure. Five words is fine.
