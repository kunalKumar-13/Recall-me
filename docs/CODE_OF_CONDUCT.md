# Code of conduct

## The short version

Be kind. Be precise. Assume good faith. Critique code, not people.
Disagreement is welcome; contempt is not.

## What we expect

- **Talk about the work.** Reviews focus on code, design, and
  evidence. "This is wrong because *X*" is fine. "You always do
  this" is not.
- **Make it easy to change your mind.** Bring data, examples, or a
  reproduction. If you don't have one yet, say so.
- **Default to written, asynchronous communication.** Most decisions
  live in PRs and issues so future contributors can read the
  reasoning.
- **Respect the project's scope.** Recall is small on purpose. PRs
  that expand scope without prior discussion will be declined
  politely; the same applies to repeated requests for features
  outside the roadmap.

## What we won't tolerate

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks against contributors,
  reviewers, or users.
- Discriminatory language or imagery on the basis of race, gender,
  sexuality, religion, disability, nationality, or any other
  protected class.
- Doxxing — publishing private information about a person without
  their consent.
- Persistent disruption of discussions or reviews.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may, at their discretion:

1. Edit or remove comments that violate this code.
2. Close PRs or issues that cannot be redirected into civil
   discussion.
3. Block accounts that repeatedly violate the code.

Decisions are documented in the relevant thread when made.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code, email
the maintainers at `conduct@recall.computer`. We will:

1. Acknowledge receipt within 72 hours.
2. Treat the report as confidential.
3. Investigate and respond.

You can also raise concerns publicly in the relevant thread if you
prefer.
